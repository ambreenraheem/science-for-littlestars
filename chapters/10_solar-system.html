Chapter 01

The Solar System 🌌

Blast off into space and discover our cosmic neighbourhood — from the blazing Sun to icy Pluto!

🔭 Let's Discover

What is the Solar System?

Our Solar System is a giant family in space — all held together by the Sun's powerful gravity.

☀️

The Sun

The Sun is a massive ball of hot gas at the centre of our Solar System. It gives us light and heat. Without the Sun, there would be no life on Earth!

🪐

8 Planets

Eight planets travel around (orbit) the Sun. They are: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune — in order from the Sun!

🌙

Moons

Many planets have moons orbiting them. Earth has 1 moon. Saturn has over 140 moons! Moons are natural satellites — they travel around planets.

☄️

Comets & Asteroids

Comets are icy space rocks that leave a glowing tail. Asteroids are rocky chunks mostly found in the Asteroid Belt between Mars and Jupiter.

💭
Think About It!

If you could travel at the speed of a plane (900 km/h), it would take you 19 years just to reach the Sun! Space is HUGE. 🚀

🌍 Planet Explorer

Meet the 8 Planets

🪐 Explore Each Planet

🔴

Mercury — The Swift Planet

Mercury is the smallest planet and the closest to the Sun. It has no atmosphere, so temperatures swing wildly — from 430°C in the day to -180°C at night! A year on Mercury (one orbit around the Sun) is just 88 Earth days.

🟡

Venus — The Hottest Planet

Venus is the hottest planet in our Solar System — even hotter than Mercury! Its thick clouds trap heat. Venus rotates backwards compared to most planets, so the Sun rises in the west on Venus. It is the brightest object in our night sky after the Moon.

🌍

Earth — Our Home

Earth is the only planet known to have life. It is perfectly distanced from the Sun — not too hot, not too cold. Earth has liquid water, a protective atmosphere, and a magnetic field that shields us from harmful solar radiation. One trip around the Sun takes 365 days.

🔴

Mars — The Red Planet

Mars looks red because its soil contains iron oxide (rust!). It has the tallest volcano in the Solar System — Olympus Mons, 3 times taller than Mount Everest! Mars has two small moons: Phobos and Deimos. Scientists are exploring Mars with robotic rovers.

🟠

Jupiter — The Giant

Jupiter is the largest planet — so big that all other planets could fit inside it! It is a gas giant with no solid surface. The famous Great Red Spot is a storm that has been raging for over 300 years! Jupiter has at least 95 known moons.

🪐

Saturn — The Ringed Planet

Saturn is famous for its beautiful rings made of ice and rock. It is the least dense planet — it would float in water! Saturn has over 140 moons, including Titan, which has its own thick atmosphere.

🔵

Uranus — The Tilted Planet

Uranus spins on its side — it is tilted at 98 degrees! This means it rolls around the Sun like a rolling ball. It is an ice giant and appears blue-green due to methane gas. It is so cold that its temperature can drop to -224°C, making it the coldest planet!

🌀

Neptune — The Windy Giant

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and has the fastest winds in the Solar System — up to 2,100 km/h! It takes 165 Earth years to complete one orbit around the Sun. Its largest moon, Triton, orbits in the opposite direction to Neptune's rotation!

🤯 WOW! The Sun makes up 99.86% of all the mass in our entire Solar System! All 8 planets, moons, asteroids and comets together only make up the remaining 0.14%. The Sun is absolutely enormous compared to everything else!
