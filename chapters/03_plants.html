Chapter 03

Amazing Plants 🌿

Plants are everywhere — in gardens, parks, forests, and even in your house! They give us food, fresh air, and beautiful flowers. Let's find out how plants live and grow! 🌱

🌱 Let's Discover

What Makes Plants Special?

Plants are amazing living things! Unlike you and me, plants can make their own food using sunlight. How cool is that? ☀️

Plants also give us the oxygen we breathe. Without plants, animals and people could not live on Earth! Let's learn about the different parts of a plant and how they work. 🌍💚 Plants are the only living organisms that can make their own food. Plants are the producers of the food chain. Plants are the base of all life on Earth.

🌞

Plants Make Their Own Food!

Plants are like little chefs — they cook their own food! 👨‍🍳

☀️ They use sunlight as energy.
💧 They drink water from the ground through their roots.
🌬️ They breathe in a gas from the air called carbon dioxide.

They mix these three things together inside their green leaves and make food! This is called photosynthesis (say it: fo-toe-SIN-thuh-sis). And guess what? While they make food, they give us fresh oxygen to breathe! 🎁 Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to create their own food (glucose) and release oxygen as a byproduct.

Fun fact: Plants are the producers of the food chain. Plants are the base of all life on Earth.

Plants are beautiful and they are very important for our planet. And we should take care of them.

🌱

Parts of a Plant

Every plant has special parts, and each one has an important job! 🛠️

🏠 Roots — grow underground and drink water from the soil. They also hold the plant in place so it doesn't fall over!
🚿 Stem — like a straw! It carries water up from the roots to the leaves and holds the plant up tall.
🍃 Leaves — the food factories! They catch sunlight and make food for the plant.
🌸 Flowers — the pretty part! They help the plant make seeds so new plants can grow.

Fun fact: Some roots, like carrots 🥕 and potatoes 🥔, are foods that we eat! Roots are the part of a plant that typically grows underground and anchors the plant in the soil. The stem is the main stalk of a plant that supports the leaves, flowers, and fruits. Leaves are the primary sites of photosynthesis in plants. Flowers are the reproductive structures of flowering plants.

🌸

Flowers, Bees & Seeds

Flowers aren't just pretty — they have a very important job! 🐝

🌺 Flowers make a sweet juice called nectar that bees and butterflies love to drink.
🐝 When bees visit flowers, they carry a yellow dust called pollen from one flower to another.
🌱 This helps the plant make seeds! Seeds can grow into brand new plants.

Fun fact: Without bees, we wouldn't have apples 🍎, strawberries 🍓, or many other fruits! Bees are super helpers! 🦸‍♀️ Pollination is the transfer of pollen from the male part of a flower to the female part of a flower, which is necessary for fertilization and the production of seeds.

🌵

So Many Kinds of Plants!

Plants come in all shapes and sizes! 🌍

🌳 Trees — big and tall, with thick trunks. Some are hundreds of years old!
🌻 Flowers — sunflowers, roses, daisies, and so many more!
🌵 Cacti — live in hot deserts and store water inside them. They have spines instead of leaves! 🏜️
🌊 Water plants — like water lilies, they float on ponds and lakes!

Fun fact: The tallest tree in the world is taller than a 35-storey building! 😮 It's called Hyperion and lives in California, USA. Trees are large, woody plants with a single main stem called a trunk, which supports branches and leaves. Flowers are the reproductive structures of flowering plants, and they come in a wide variety of shapes, sizes, and colors. Cacti are succulent plants that are adapted to arid environments, and they have spines instead of leaves to reduce water loss. Water plants are plants that grow in water, and they have adaptations that allow them to survive in aquatic environments.

💚

Why We Need Plants

Plants do so many wonderful things for us and our planet! 🌎

🫁 They give us oxygen to breathe — fresh air every day!
🍎 They give us food — fruits, vegetables, grains, and nuts!
🏡 They give us wood to build houses and make paper!
🐦 They give animals homes and food too!

Fun fact: We should take care of plants and trees — they take care of us! 🌳❤️ Oxygen is a gas that is essential for the survival of most living organisms, including humans. Food is any substance that provides nutritional support for an organism. Wood is a porous and fibrous structural tissue found in the stems and roots of trees and other woody plants. Animals are multicellular, eukaryotic organisms that are typically motile and heterotrophic.

💭
Think About It!

Without plants, there would be no fresh air to breathe! Every time you take a breath, you're breathing oxygen that plants made for you. Plants are like the Earth's air cleaners! 🌿🫁 Can you think of how many plants you see every day?

How Do Plants Grow?

🌱

Step 1: A Tiny Seed

Every big plant starts as a tiny seed! 🤏 When a seed is placed in soil and gets water and warmth, something magical happens — a little root pokes out the bottom and pushes into the soil. Then a tiny green shoot pushes up toward the sunlight! 🌤️ This is called germination (say it: jer-mih-NAY-shun). It's like the seed is waking up! 😊 Germination is the process by which a plant grows from a seed. It begins when the seed absorbs water and swells, causing the seed coat to split open. The embryo inside the seed then begins to grow, with the root emerging first and growing downward into the soil, followed by the shoot emerging and growing upward toward the sunlight.

🌿

Step 2: Leaves Open Up

Once the little shoot is above the ground, its first leaves open up to catch sunlight! ☀️ The leaves are green because of something called chlorophyll (say it: KLOR-oh-fill) — it's the special green stuff that helps plants catch light. Now the plant can start making its own food! 🍽️ The roots keep drinking water, and the stem grows taller and taller! 📏 Leaves are the primary sites of photosynthesis in plants. Chlorophyll is a green pigment found in plants that absorbs sunlight and is essential for photosynthesis.

🌸

Step 3: Flowers Bloom!

When the plant is big enough, it grows beautiful flowers! 🌷 Flowers are colourful and smell lovely to attract bees 🐝 and butterflies 🦋. When a bee lands on a flower to drink nectar, pollen sticks to its fuzzy body. When it flies to the next flower, it drops some pollen there — this is called pollination! It helps the plant make seeds. 🌟 Pollination is the transfer of pollen from the male part of a flower to the female part of a flower. It is a necessary step in the reproduction of flowering plants.

🍎

Step 4: Fruits & Seeds

After pollination, the flower slowly turns into a fruit! 🍓 Inside the fruit are seeds. The fruit protects the seeds and makes them tasty so animals will eat them! 🐿️ When animals eat the fruit, they carry the seeds to new places — and new plants can grow there! That's nature's clever way of spreading plants all around. 🌍✨ The process of pollination can be summarized by the following equation: Flower + Pollen → Fruit + Seeds

🌳

Step 5: Growing Big & Strong

Plants keep growing their whole lives! 🌱➡️🌳 Tiny seedlings can become giant trees! Trees grow rings inside their trunk — one ring for every year they live. You can count the rings to see how old a tree is! Some trees are thousands of years old — older than castles and pyramids! 🏰 Plants always grow toward the light because they need it to make food. Smart, right? 💡 The process of photosynthesis can be summarized by the following equation: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Light Energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ Where CO₂ is carbon dioxide, H₂O is water, C₆H₁₂O₆ is glucose (sugar), and O₂ is oxygen.

🤯 WOW! One big tree can make enough oxygen for two people to breathe for a whole year! 🌳🫁 Trees also clean the air by soaking up dirty gases. That's why forests are called the "lungs of the Earth"! We should plant more trees and take care of the ones we have. 🌍💚
