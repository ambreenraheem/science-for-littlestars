Chapter 02

Amazing Plants 🌿

Discover how plants make food from sunlight, grow from seeds, and why they're vital for all life on Earth!

🌱 Let's Discover

What Makes Plants Special?

Plants are living things that can make their own food! They grow all around us — from tiny mosses to giant trees.

🌞

Photosynthesis

Plants use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide from the air to make their own food. This amazing process is called photosynthesis. The green substance in leaves called chlorophyll captures the sunlight!

🌱

Roots, Stem & Leaves

Roots absorb water and minerals from soil. The stem carries water up to the leaves. Leaves are the food factories — they catch sunlight and make food for the whole plant!

🌸

Flowers & Seeds

Flowers help plants reproduce. They attract bees and butterflies to help with pollination. After pollination, flowers form seeds. Seeds grow into new plants!

🌵

Types of Plants

There are many types of plants: flowering plants, trees, ferns, mosses and cacti. Some are evergreen (keep their leaves all year), others are deciduous (shed leaves in winter).

💭
Think About It!

Without plants, there would be NO oxygen to breathe! Every time you take a breath, you're breathing oxygen that plants have made. We owe our lives to plants! 🌿

🌱

Germination

A seed needs water, warmth and air to sprout. This is called germination. The seed absorbs water, swells up, and a tiny root pushes out first — then a shoot grows upward toward the light!

🌿

Photosynthesis

Inside every green leaf, tiny structures called chloroplasts capture sunlight. They use this energy to turn water (from roots) and carbon dioxide (from air) into glucose (sugar) — the plant's food! Oxygen is released as a bonus gift to us.

🌸

Pollination

Pollen from one flower must reach another flower for seeds to form. Bees, butterflies, birds and even wind help carry pollen from flower to flower. This is pollination — and it's why we have fruits and vegetables!

🍎

Fruits & Seeds

After pollination, the flower's ovary swells into a fruit that contains seeds. The fruit protects seeds and helps spread them. Animals eat fruits and deposit seeds elsewhere through their droppings — clever seed dispersal!

🌳

Growing Up

As plants grow, they develop a complex system of roots underground and branches above. Trees can live for thousands of years — the oldest tree known is over 5,000 years old! Plants grow toward light in a process called phototropism.

🤯 WOW! A single large tree can absorb 22 kg of carbon dioxide per year and produce enough oxygen for 2 people to breathe! Forests are Earth's lungs — that's why protecting trees is so important.
