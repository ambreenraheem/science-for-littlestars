Chapter 05

Mighty Magnets 🧲

Discover the invisible force that pushes and pulls — magnetism is one of the most fascinating forces in nature!

🧲 Let's Discover

What is a Magnet?

A magnet is an object that creates an invisible force called a magnetic field. This force can attract or repel certain materials — without even touching them!

🧲

Magnetic Materials

Magnets attract materials that contain iron, nickel or cobalt. These are called magnetic materials. Plastic, wood, glass and rubber are NOT attracted to magnets — they are non-magnetic.

🔵🔴

North & South Poles

Every magnet has two poles: North (N) and South (S). Opposite poles attract (N pulls S), but same poles repel (N pushes N away). You can never separate the two poles — even if you cut a magnet in half!

🌍

Earth is a Giant Magnet!

Earth has a huge magnetic field created by molten iron in its core. This field protects us from harmful solar radiation. It also helps animals like birds and sea turtles navigate using Earth's magnetic field!

⚡

Electromagnets

An electromagnet is made by wrapping wire around iron and passing electricity through it. The electricity creates a magnetic field. When you turn the electricity off, the magnet turns off! These are used in cranes, motors and MRI machines.

💭
Think About It!

Compasses work because Earth is a giant magnet! The compass needle is a small magnet, and its north pole is attracted to Earth's magnetic south pole (near the geographic North Pole). This has guided sailors and explorers for hundreds of years! 🧭

🔵

Magnetic Field

A magnetic field is the invisible area around a magnet where its force works. If you sprinkle iron filings around a magnet, they line up along field lines — forming a beautiful pattern that shows the invisible force! The field is strongest at the poles.

🔄

Attract & Repel

Place two magnets with opposite poles together — they snap together (attract!). Flip one around so the same poles face — they push each other away (repel!). This push and pull is the basis of how electric motors and generators work.

🗺️

The Compass

A compass needle is a tiny magnet balanced to spin freely. Earth's magnetic field pulls the needle's north pole toward magnetic north. Ancient Chinese navigators were using compasses over 2,000 years ago — one of humanity's greatest inventions!

⚡

Making an Electromagnet

You can make an electromagnet at home! Wrap copper wire around a large iron nail about 30 times, then connect the wire ends to a battery. The nail becomes magnetic and can pick up paper clips! Disconnect the battery and the magnetism disappears.

🏥

Magnets in Everyday Life

Magnets are everywhere! They're in your headphones, speakers, hard drives, refrigerator doors, electric motors, MRI hospital scanners, and maglev trains that float above their tracks at 600 km/h using magnetic repulsion!

🤯 WOW! If you travel to the magnetic north pole, a compass will point straight DOWN into the ground — not forward! And the magnetic poles are slowly moving. The magnetic north pole moves about 50 km per year and has actually completely flipped in Earth's history, over 180 times!
