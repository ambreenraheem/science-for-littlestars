Chapter 04

The Five Senses 👁️

Your senses are your superpowers! They help you explore the world around you every single moment.

👁️ Let's Discover

Your Five Amazing Senses

Sight, hearing, smell, taste and touch — your five senses send information to your brain so you can understand the world around you!

👁️

Sight

Your eyes collect light and send signals to your brain to form pictures. Your eyes can see over 10 million different colours! Pupils get bigger in the dark to let in more light and smaller in bright light.

👂

Hearing

Sound travels as waves. Your outer ear catches these waves and funnels them to the eardrum, which vibrates. These vibrations are turned into signals that your brain understands as sound.

👃

Smell

Your nose can detect over 1 trillion different smells! Tiny hair-like sensors inside your nose pick up odour molecules from the air and send messages to your brain. Smell is our most powerful memory trigger.

👅

Taste & Touch

Taste buds on your tongue detect sweet, sour, salty, bitter and umami (savoury). Your skin is your largest sense organ — it detects touch, pressure, temperature and pain to keep you safe!

💭
Think About It!

Did you know taste and smell work together? When you have a blocked nose, food seems to have less flavour! About 80% of what we think we 'taste' is actually what we 'smell'. Try tasting food while holding your nose! 👃

👁️

How Your Eyes Work

Light enters your eye through the pupil and is focused by the lens onto the retina — a screen at the back of your eye. The retina has 120 million rod cells (for dim light) and 6 million cone cells (for colour). These send signals via the optic nerve to your brain!

👂

How Your Ears Work

Sound waves travel into your ear canal and vibrate the eardrum. Three tiny bones — the hammer, anvil and stirrup — amplify the vibration. The cochlea, a coiled tube filled with fluid, converts vibrations into electrical signals for the brain.

👃

How Your Nose Works

When you breathe in, odour molecules land on the olfactory epithelium — a patch of special cells high in your nose. These cells send signals along the olfactory nerve directly to your brain's smell centre, which is also linked to memory and emotion!

👅

How Taste Works

Your tongue has about 10,000 taste buds! Each taste bud contains receptor cells that detect chemicals in food and send signals to your brain. Babies have taste buds not just on their tongue but on the roof of their mouth too!

✋

How Touch Works

Your skin contains millions of receptors that respond to touch, pressure, heat, cold and pain. Fingertips are extremely sensitive — they have up to 3,000 touch receptors per square centimetre! Pain is a warning signal that protects your body from harm.

🤯 WOW! Your nose is directly wired to the memory and emotion part of your brain. That's why a smell can instantly transport you back to a memory — like smelling fresh bread and remembering your grandmother's kitchen. No other sense has this direct connection!
