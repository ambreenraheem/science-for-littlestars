Chapter 06

Wild Weather 🌦️

Rain, thunder, snow and sunshine — weather affects every living thing on Earth. Let's explore the science behind it!

🌦️ Let's Discover

What is Weather?

Weather describes what the atmosphere (the air around Earth) is doing right now — sunshine, rain, wind, clouds, snow or storms!

☁️

How Clouds Form

Water from oceans, lakes and rivers evaporates (turns into water vapour) and rises into the sky. As it cools, it condenses into tiny droplets that form clouds. When droplets get heavy enough, they fall as rain or snow!

🌊

The Water Cycle

Water constantly moves between the sea, sky and land in a cycle: Evaporation → Condensation → Precipitation → Collection. This cycle has been going for 4 billion years — the water you drink was once dinosaur sweat!

💨

Wind

Wind is moving air. It is caused by differences in air pressure. Warm air rises (creating low pressure), and cool air rushes in to replace it. The greater the pressure difference, the stronger the wind!

🌪️

Extreme Weather

Hurricanes are massive rotating storms that form over warm oceans. Tornadoes are powerful spinning columns of air. Blizzards are severe snowstorms. Lightning is a giant spark of electricity between clouds or cloud and ground!

💭
Think About It!

The same water has been cycling around Earth for billions of years. The water in your glass today might have once been in a cloud over ancient Egypt, fallen as rain in a rainforest, or been drunk by a dinosaur! Water is never lost — it just changes form. 💧

🌡️

Temperature

Temperature tells us how hot or cold the air is. The thermometer measures it in degrees Celsius (°C) or Fahrenheit (°F). The hottest temperature ever recorded on Earth was 56.7°C in Death Valley, USA. The coldest was -89.2°C in Antarctica!

☁️

Types of Clouds

Cumulus clouds are fluffy like cotton balls — usually means fair weather. Cumulonimbus are giant storm clouds that bring thunder and lightning. Stratus clouds are flat grey sheets that bring drizzle. Cirrus clouds are thin and wispy, high up in the sky.

🌈

Rainbow Science

A rainbow appears when sunlight passes through water droplets in the air. The droplets act like tiny prisms, splitting white light into its 7 colours: Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, Indigo, Violet. You always see a rainbow opposite the Sun!

⛈️

Lightning & Thunder

Lightning is a giant electrical discharge — a spark! It happens when electric charges build up in storm clouds. Lightning heats the air to 30,000°C (5 times hotter than the Sun's surface!), causing it to rapidly expand — that bang is thunder. Light travels faster than sound, so you see lightning before hearing thunder.

❄️

Snow & Ice

Snow forms when water vapour in clouds freezes directly into ice crystals. Every snowflake has a unique six-sided shape — no two are exactly alike! Hail forms when raindrops are carried up by strong winds, freeze, gather layers of ice, and fall as lumps.

🤯 WOW! A single hurricane can release 200 times more energy per day than the entire world's electrical generating capacity! And lightning strikes Earth about 100 times every second — that's 8 million lightning bolts per day worldwide!
