Chapter 04

Amazing Animals 🦁

Animals live everywhere — in jungles, oceans, deserts, and maybe even in your backyard! Let's meet some amazing animal groups and learn what makes each one special. 🐾

🦁 Let's Discover

The Animal Kingdom

There are millions of different kinds of animals on Earth! 🌍 Some are big like elephants 🐘, and some are tiny like ants 🐜. Some live in the sea, some fly in the sky, and some live on land.

Scientists put animals into groups based on what they look like and how they live. Let's explore the most amazing groups! 🔍

🦁

Mammals

Mammals are animals that have fur or hair and feed their babies milk — just like your mum fed you when you were a baby! 🍼

🐶 Dogs, cats, and horses are mammals!
🐬 Even dolphins and whales are mammals — they breathe air, not water!
🦇 Bats are mammals too — the only ones that can fly!

Fun fact: YOU are a mammal! You have hair, you're warm, and you were fed milk as a baby. Mammals are warm-blooded vertebrates characterized by the presence of mammary glands, which in females produce milk for nourishing the young.

🐦

Birds

Birds have feathers and wings, and most of them can fly! 🕊️ They lay eggs and keep them warm until the babies hatch. 🥚

🦅 Eagles can see super far — much better than humans!
🐧 Penguins are birds that can't fly — but they're amazing swimmers! 🏊
🦜 Parrots can learn to copy what you say! "Hello!" 👋

Fun fact: The hummingbird is the smallest bird in the world — it's tinier than your hand! 🤏 Birds are warm-blooded vertebrates characterized by the presence of feathers, wings, and a beak, and most are capable of flight.

🐟

Fish

Fish live in water — in rivers, lakes, and oceans! 🌊 They breathe using special parts called gills (not lungs like us!). They have fins to swim and scales to protect their bodies.

🐡 Pufferfish can blow up like a balloon when they're scared!
🦈 Sharks have been around longer than dinosaurs!
🐠 Clownfish live inside sea plants called anemones — like Nemo! 🎬

Fun fact: Some fish can glow in the dark deep down in the ocean! 💡🐟 Fish are aquatic vertebrates that have gills for breathing, fins for movement, and scales for protection.

🦎

Reptiles & Amphibians

Reptiles have dry, scaly skin — like lizards 🦎, snakes 🐍, turtles 🐢, and crocodiles 🐊! They lay eggs and love to warm up in the sun. ☀️

Amphibians like frogs 🐸 and salamanders can live in water AND on land! Frogs start life as tiny tadpoles swimming in ponds, and then they grow legs and hop onto land! 🐸➡️🦵

Fun fact: Chameleons can change their skin colour to match what's around them — like a magic trick! 🎨✨ Reptiles are cold-blooded vertebrates characterized by the presence of scales, and most lay eggs on land. Amphibians are cold-blooded vertebrates that can live both in water and on land, and they typically undergo metamorphosis from an aquatic larval stage to a terrestrial adult stage.

🐛

Insects & Bugs

Insects are the biggest group of animals on Earth! They all have 6 legs and many have wings. 🪰

🐝 Bees make honey and help flowers grow!
🦋 Butterflies start as caterpillars and then grow beautiful wings!
🐜 Ants work in big teams — they can carry things much heavier than themselves!
🐞 Ladybirds are tiny but they help farmers by eating plant pests!

Fun fact: There are more insects on Earth than any other kind of animal! 🌍🐜🐜🐜 Insects are invertebrates characterized by the presence of six legs, a three-part body (head, thorax, and abdomen), and usually one or two pairs of wings.

💭
Think About It!

Did you know that for every ONE person on Earth, there are about 1 billion insects? 🐜 Insects may be tiny, but they do HUGE jobs — bees help flowers grow, worms make the soil healthy, and ladybirds protect plants. Tiny but mighty! 💪

What Do Animals Eat?

🦁

Meat Eaters (Carnivores)

Some animals only eat meat — they are called carnivores (say it: KAR-nih-vors). 🥩 Lions, sharks, eagles, and wolves are all carnivores! They have sharp teeth and strong claws to help them catch their food. Lions are called the "King of the Jungle" because they are such powerful hunters! 👑🦁 Carnivores are animals that eat only meat. Some examples of carnivores are lions, tigers, sharks, and eagles.

🐘

Plant Eaters (Herbivores)

Some animals only eat plants — they are called herbivores (say it: HER-bih-vors). 🌿 Elephants, cows, rabbits, and horses all eat plants! They have flat teeth to chew and grind leaves and grass. An elephant eats so many plants that it spends about 16 hours a day just eating! 🐘🍃 That's almost all day long! Herbivores are animals that eat only plants. Some examples of herbivores are elephants, cows, rabbits, and horses.

🐻

Both! (Omnivores)

Some animals eat both plants AND meat — they are called omnivores (say it: OM-nih-vors). 🍎🥩 Bears love to eat berries AND fish! Pigs eat almost anything — fruits, vegetables, and insects! 🐷 And guess what? Humans are omnivores too! That's why you can eat a yummy salad AND a tasty chicken nugget. 🥗🍗 Omnivores are animals that eat both plants and meat. Some examples of omnivores are bears, pigs, and humans.

🦋

Amazing Changes!

Some animals completely change their shape as they grow up! A fuzzy caterpillar 🐛 wraps itself in a little sleeping bag and comes out as a beautiful butterfly! 🦋 This amazing change takes about 2 weeks! A tiny tadpole swimming in a pond slowly grows legs and turns into a frog! 🐸 Nature is full of surprises! Metamorphosis is a biological process by which an animal physically develops after birth or hatching, involving a conspicuous and relatively abrupt change in the animal's body structure through cell growth and differentiation.

🐻‍❄️

What Animals Do in Winter

When it gets really cold in winter, animals have clever ways to survive! ❄️ Some animals take a very long sleep called hibernation — like bears 🐻 and hedgehogs 🦔. They sleep for months and use energy stored in their bodies! Other animals fly to warmer places — this is called migration. Birds 🐦 fly thousands of kilometres to find sunshine and food! ☀️ Hibernation is a state of inactivity and metabolic depression in endotherms. It is a survival strategy used by many animals to conserve energy during periods of cold temperatures and limited food availability. Migration is the seasonal movement of animals from one region to another. It is a survival strategy used by many animals to find food, water, and shelter.

🤯 WOW! The blue whale is the biggest animal that has EVER lived🐋. It can be as long as three school buses put together! And its heart is so big that it's the size of a small car! 🚗💙 Imagine a heart THAT big! The blue whale weighing up to 200 tons and measuring up to 30 meters in length. It is a marine mammal that feeds on krill, small crustaceans that live in the ocean.
