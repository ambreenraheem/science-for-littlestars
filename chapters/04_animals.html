Chapter 03

Amazing Animals 🦁

From the deepest oceans to the highest mountains — the animal kingdom is full of incredible creatures!

🦁 Let's Discover

The Animal Kingdom

Animals are living things that can move, eat and breathe. There are millions of species on Earth — each with amazing adaptations for survival!

🦁

Mammals

Mammals are warm-blooded animals that feed their babies milk. They have hair or fur. Humans, dogs, elephants, dolphins and bats are all mammals. Most give birth to live young.

🐦

Birds

Birds have feathers and wings (though not all can fly!). They are warm-blooded and lay eggs. Penguins cannot fly but are expert swimmers. Eagles can see 8 times better than humans!

🐟

Fish

Fish live in water and breathe through gills. They are cold-blooded — their body temperature matches their surroundings. Fish have scales and use fins to swim.

🦎

Reptiles & Amphibians

Reptiles (like snakes and lizards) have dry scaly skin. Amphibians (like frogs) can live both in water and on land. Frogs start life as tadpoles in water and later grow legs!

💭
Think About It!

Insects are the most numerous animals on Earth! For every 1 person, there are about 1.4 billion insects. They may be tiny, but insects pollinate our crops, decompose waste, and form the base of many food chains. 🐛

🦁

Carnivores

Carnivores eat only meat. They have sharp teeth and claws for hunting. Lions, eagles, sharks and wolves are carnivores. Their bodies are built for speed, stealth, and catching prey.

🐘

Herbivores

Herbivores eat only plants. They often have flat teeth for grinding. Elephants, cows, rabbits and butterflies are herbivores. An elephant eats up to 150 kg of plants per day!

🐻

Omnivores

Omnivores eat both plants and animals. This gives them a flexible diet — great for survival! Bears, pigs, crows and humans are omnivores. We can eat a huge variety of foods.

🦋

Metamorphosis

Some animals go through dramatic changes called metamorphosis. A caterpillar turns into a butterfly inside a chrysalis. This amazing transformation takes about 2 weeks! A tadpole becomes a frog over several months.

🐻‍❄️

Hibernation & Migration

Some animals sleep through winter (hibernate) to survive cold and food shortages. Bears, hedgehogs and bats hibernate. Others migrate — like birds flying thousands of kilometres to warmer places for winter.

🤯 WOW! The blue whale is the largest animal that has ever lived on Earth — bigger than any dinosaur! It can grow to 30 metres long and its heart is the size of a small car. You could crawl through its arteries!
